01

What VEKTRA is

"The graph is
the instrument."
1 signal type
847ms typical latency
48k sample resolution

VEKTRA is not a DAW. It is not a creative suite. It is not a plugin.

It is closer to a language — a way of thinking about time, signal, and form simultaneously. Most tools separate the visual from the sonic from the logical. VEKTRA treats them as one graph.

Every parameter in the system is a node. Every node can be connected to every other. When you modulate a frequency, that modulation can drive a visual shader, rewrite a generative rule, or trigger a physical output. Everything is inspectable, forkable, and live.

02

The three primitives

01 / signal

Everything is a signal

Audio, video, MIDI, mouse position, and sensor data share a single type: Signal<T>. Any signal drives any other. The system does not distinguish between the domain of sound and the domain of image — both are time-varying functions.

02 / graph

Patches are programs

A VEKTRA patch is a directed graph — nodes are signal processors, edges are signal paths. Write patches in the visual editor or in vex, VEKTRA's text language. Both representations are live-editable. A running patch can rewrite itself.

03 / time

Time is explicit

Every signal carries an explicit timestamp at sample resolution. Latency is a value you can inspect and route — not a hidden variable. Compose works precise to ±1 sample, synchronize across machines, or use time as a compositional parameter.
