01

What VEKTRA is

VEKTRA is not a DAW. It is not a creative suite. It is not a plugin.

It is closer to a language — a way of thinking about time, signal, and form simultaneously. Most creative tools separate the visual from the sonic from the logical. VEKTRA treats them as one graph.

Every parameter in the system is a node. Every node can be connected to every other. When you modulate a frequency, that modulation can drive a visual shader, rewrite a generative rule, or trigger a physical output. The graph is the instrument.

"Everything is inspectable,
forkable, and live."

VEKTRA was built by a sound engineer and a computer vision researcher who spent a residency year at Spinnerei Leipzig asking a single question: why do the tools for time-based art treat time as an afterthought?

The answer became VEKTRA.

02

The three primitives

01 / signal

Everything is a signal

In VEKTRA, audio waveforms, video pixel buffers, MIDI note streams, mouse trajectories, and sensor data share a single type: Signal<T>. Any signal can drive any other. The system does not distinguish between the domain of sound and the domain of image — they are both time-varying functions.

02 / graph

Patches are programs

A VEKTRA patch is a directed graph — nodes are signal processors, edges are signal paths. You can write patches in the visual editor or in the text language vex. Both representations are live-editable. A running patch can rewrite itself. Patches can be forked, versioned, and diffed like code.

03 / time

Time is explicit

Every signal in VEKTRA carries an explicit timestamp at sample resolution. Latency is a value you can inspect and route, not a hidden variable. This means you can compose works that are precise to ±1 sample, synchronize across machines over a network, or deliberately introduce time as a compositional parameter.

Who uses it

01 · composer

Mara Voss

Electronic composer working at the intersection of algorithmic composition and live performance. Uses VEKTRA to write compositions where the score rewrites itself in response to audience biometrics and room acoustics. Premiered at CTM Festival, Berlin 2025.
