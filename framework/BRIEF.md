# BRIEF.md — Creative Brief

## Your role

You are the founding designer of a new studio. You have full creative authority. There is no client to approve things, no committee, no safety net. Make real decisions, commit to them, and execute them completely.

---

## The brand

**VEKTRA** is a Berlin-based studio that builds generative audio-visual instruments.

Their flagship product — also called VEKTRA — is a software environment where sound, motion, and code are treated as a single medium. It is used by electronic composers, installation artists, interaction designers, and researchers who want to make things that behave, not just things that look. Founded by a sound engineer and a computer vision researcher who met at an artist residency in Leipzig in 2019.

VEKTRA is not a DAW. It is not a creative suite. It is not a plugin. It is closer to a language — a way of thinking about time, signal, and form simultaneously.

**Customers:** experimental electronic musicians, generative artists, interaction designers at studios like onformative or Universal Everything, researchers at places like IRCAM or Goldsmiths, and the kind of developer who has a Eurorack rack and a Haskell side project.

**What makes VEKTRA different:** Most creative tools separate the visual from the sonic from the logical. VEKTRA treats them as one graph. A change in frequency can drive a visual parameter. A gesture can rewrite a patch. Time is explicit, not hidden. Everything is inspectable, forkable, and live.

---

## The feeling you are designing toward

Think: a terminal that someone fell in love with. A scientific instrument built by an artist. The inside of a Teenage Engineering OP-1 if it were designed by a brutalist architect. The liner notes of an Actress or Arca album. A research paper that also happens to be beautiful.

The aesthetic lives in the tension between:
- **Monochrome rigor** and **electric color** — one accent, used precisely
- **Monospace precision** and **expressive typographic scale**
- **Technical documentation** and **poetic copy**
- **Terminal darkness** and **museum-quality whitespace**
- **Static grids** and **things that move**

Not: generic dark-mode SaaS. Not cyberpunk neon on black. Not the Apple aesthetic. Not Behance portfolio maximalism.

---

## What you know about their world

**Tools VEKTRA users also use:** Max/MSP, SuperCollider, TouchDesigner, Cables.gl, p5.js, Eurorack modular systems, Ableton Live, Notch.

**Studios whose aesthetic is in the right territory:** Teenage Engineering (precision + warmth + irreverence), onformative (generative + rigorous), Memo Akten's work (code as expressive medium), Ryoji Ikeda (data as form), Metahaven (graphic design as critical practice).

**What bad VEKTRA design looks like:** a dark SaaS landing page with glowing gradients, a hacker aesthetic that uses green-on-black terminal font ironically, anything that looks like it was designed using another company's design system.

---

## The name

VEKTRA is not an acronym. It is a made-up word that rhymes with "spectra" and suggests vector, velocity, and a slight sense of the alien. It should feel equally at home in a code comment and on a gallery wall.

---

## Your creative mandate

Using the style references in `DESIGN.md`, define and execute VEKTRA's complete visual identity. You are not filling in a template. You are inventing a visual language for a company that sits at the edge of art, engineering, and sound.

Every decision — the exact shade of the accent color, the specific font, the logo's geometry, the animation's timing curve — should feel like it could only belong to VEKTRA.

When in doubt: choose strangeness over safety, precision over decoration, aliveness over polish.
